Marina Bay Sands Casino Review Jump to comments

Marina Bay Sands is a casino resort complex on Marina Bay in Singapore. It is the world's most expensive stand alone casino and represents a US$5.7 billion investment by Las Vegas Sands Corporation. The place is opulent and huge with floor space covering 6,253,000 square feet on almost 40 acres of prime real estate. The property features a luxury hotel with over 2,500 rooms and suites, over a million square feet of convention and exhibition space, theatres, entertainment venues, a museum, and over 800,000 square feet of space devoted to retailers and restaurants. There are  50 restaurants including 9 fine dining establishments.

At about  160,000 square feet, the casino covers four levels of gaming floor-space and offers about 2,500 gaming machines on the first three levels and 700 gaming tables spread over all four floors- a ratio only seen in Asian establishments. There are 13 different game types for table players with popular game variations such as no commission on some of the baccarat tables. Visitors are allowed free entry to the casino but Singaporeans must pay the equivalent of $100 for every 24 hours of access. Monthly passes can be purchased for a substantial discount.

In addition to vast casino holdings in the US, Las Vegas Sands Corporation also owns several Asian properties. Through their majority-owned subsidiary Sands China Ltd., the company owns and operates casino resorts on Macao's Cotai Strip, including Venetian Casino Resort Macao, Four Seasons Hotel Macao, and Sands Cotai Central . The company also owns and operates the Sands Macao on the Macao Peninsula.

Gaming at Marina Bay Sands

With three levels of gaming floor space with about 2,500 slot machines,  Marina Bay Sands offers a wide selection of the newest and most popular gaming machines in the world.  Players can choose from  a vast range of betting levels for video slots, reel slots, and multi-station electronic games such as Roulette and Sic-Bo.

With more than 13 varieties of table games on all four levels of gaming space, (only one of which is a non smoking level) players will find games such as baccarat, roulette, sic-bo, Singapore stud poker, non commission baccarat, and the easy to play Money Wheel. There are about 500 tables in the main areas with a variety of betting limits. High rollers will enjoy another 200 gaming tables in the High Limit, Ruby, and Paiza areas You will find these games in exclusive salons on the upper levels of the casino for the most part.

Singapore Museum at Marina Bay Sands

Museum

The SkyPark at Marina Bay Sands Singapore is a true marvel overlooking the seaside.
